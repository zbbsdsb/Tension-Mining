### Option 1: For AI Engineers

🧵 Most people search for solutions. Great researchers search for tensions. I built Tension Mining — a 7-phase research methodology that discovers cross-domain invariants by mining tensions. Here's how it works and why it matters 🧵

---

Phase 1-3: Collect real-world phenomena → Identify ineliminable tensions → Extract cross-domain invariants. No assumptions, no solutions-first thinking. Just patterns that keep showing up across biology, economics, and technology.

---

Phase 4-6: Study how reality resolves these tensions (mechanisms) → Combine into a coherent model → Derive algorithms from the mechanisms (not from first principles). The algorithm feels inevitable.

---

Phase 7: Destruction. Attack your own model. Find weak assumptions, missing tensions, and failure modes. The strongest models survive their own destruction. Most people skip this step. That's why most models fail.
