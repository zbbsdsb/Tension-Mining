Phase 01+
Phenomenon Mining

Observe reality before building abstractions. Collect 5-10 real-world examples from at least 3 distinct domains. The goal is to see what systems actually do, not what you assume they do.

Gate: 5+ phenomena from 3+ domains
Phase 02+
Tension Mining

Identify forces pulling the system in different directions. These are ineliminable tradeoffs, not "pros vs cons." Each tension must have a clear Force A and Force B.

Gate: 5+ tensions with Force A/B identified
Phase 03+
Invariant Mining

Extract principles that appear across multiple tensions and domains. These are the "laws" of complex systems — patterns that hold regardless of the specific domain.

Gate: 3+ cross-domain invariants
Phase 04+
Mechanism Mining

Study how reality resolves tensions. Nature, society, and technology have already developed solutions. Don't invent — discover what already works.

Gate: 3+ mechanisms mapped to tensions
Phase 05+
System Synthesis

Combine tensions, invariants, and mechanisms into a coherent model with predicted behaviors. This is where everything connects.

Gate: Coherent model with failure modes
Phase 06+
Algorithm Synthesis

Only now design algorithms. Derive them from the mechanisms discovered in Phase 4, not from first principles. The algorithm should feel inevitable.

Gate: Algorithms derived from mechanisms
Phase 07+
Destruction

Attack the model. Assume it is wrong. Identify weak assumptions, missing tensions, and possible redesigns. The strongest models survive their own destruction.

Gate: Failure analysis + D1-D5 evaluation

Why Tension Mining

Not Another Methodology

Frameworks you already know answer "how to solve." Tension Mining answers "what forces shape this problem before you solve it."

Alternative
Design Thinking
Solution-oriented. Starts from "how might we." Empathy maps find user needs, but miss the structural forces that constrain those needs.
Tension Mining adds: Starts from "what forces cannot be eliminated." Finds the permanent tradeoffs that design cannot dissolve.
Alternative
Systems Dynamics
Domain-specific and quantitative. Requires calibrated models and historical data. Powerful within a domain, but each model starts from zero.
Tension Mining adds: Cross-domain invariants that transfer between domains. The phenomena change; the tensions persist.
Alternative
Cynefin Framework
Classifies problems (Simple, Complicated, Complex, Chaotic) but does not prescribe a discovery process for the Complex domain.
Tension Mining adds: A structured 7-phase pipeline purpose-built for Complex domain analysis, with gate conditions ensuring rigor.
Alternative
First Principles
Decomposes a problem into its fundamental constituents. Powerful but open-ended — no guidance on which constituents matter.
Tension Mining adds: Guides decomposition toward ineliminable tensions — forces that cannot be decomposed away, only managed.

Featured Demonstration

PageRank Through the Pipeline

Watch how Google's PageRank emerges from the full 7-phase Tension Mining pipeline — from observed phenomena to algorithmic derivation.

01Phenomenon Mining
Power-Law Link Structure
Web links follow a power-law distribution: a few pages (Google, Yahoo) accumulate millions of inbound links while most pages have near-zero. Users create links as implicit quality votes.
02Tension Mining
Local vs Global · Freedom vs Efficiency
Each page sees only its own links but needs global relevance. Anyone can link anywhere, making the web noisy yet rich. Both tensions are ineliminable — they define the problem space.
03Invariant Mining
Local Rules Create Global Order
Local link decisions, aggregated across the entire web, reveal a stable global ranking. Gradients Drive Movement: rank diffuses through the link structure like heat through a conducting material.
04Mechanism Mining
Eigenvector Centrality
A page's importance is the sum of its inbound neighbors' importance. This self-consistent recursive definition — $r_i = \sum_{j \in B_i} r_j / N_j$ — is the mathematical mechanism.
05System Synthesis
Iterative Power Method
The PageRank algorithm computes the principal eigenvector of the web's link matrix via repeated iteration. A damping factor (d=0.85) models random surfing and guarantees convergence.
06Algorithm Synthesis
Distributed Computation
Sparse matrix-vector multiplication enables processing across billions of pages via MapReduce. Each iteration runs in parallel on web-scale data. Converges within approximately 50 rounds.
07Destruction
Link Farms and SEO Manipulation
Artificial link structures exploit the algorithm's trust in link topology as relevance signal. Google's countermeasures: nofollow attribute, Penguin algorithm, and human quality raters.
Key Insight

The PageRank algorithm was not designed — it was discovered through the pipeline. Each phase narrowed the design space until the mathematical structure became inevitable. This is the promise of Tension Mining: not creativity, but inevitability.

Quality Standard

D1-D5 Quality Rubric

Every analysis is self-scored across five dimensions. Mastery means your analysis passes the Destruction Phase — not that it is complete.

Dimension D1 — Aware D3 — Competent D5 — Master
Depth
Domain coverage
 Single domain. Phenomena from one context only. 2-3 domains. Cross-domain patterns beginning to emerge. 5+ domains. Patterns validated across unrelated fields.
Breadth
Tension coverage
 2 tensions identified. Surface-level tradeoffs. 5 tensions identified. Include at least one surprising tension. 10+ tensions identified. Full atlas coverage with [CORE] labels.
Rigor
Validation depth
 Intuition-based. Claims supported by single phenomenon. Cross-validated. Each claim supported by 2+ independent sources. Falsification attempts documented. Negative results included.
Novelty
Insight quality
 Known patterns. Confirms what experts already know. Cross-domain insight. Connects previously unrelated fields. Surprising invariants. Contradicts common assumptions.
Actionability
Output utility
 Abstract insight. Interesting but no design implications. Design implications. Suggests specific tradeoff resolution strategies. Direct algorithm derivation. Mechanism maps to computational structure.
