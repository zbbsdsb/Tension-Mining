The Methodology

Seven Phases. Strict Order. No Skipping.

Each phase has a gate condition. You cannot proceed until it is met. The AI interviews you one question at a time. You drive the content; the methodology enforces the discipline.

01

Phenomenon Mining

Observe reality before building abstractions. Collect 5-10 real-world examples from at least 3 distinct domains — ant colonies, markets, cities, ecosystems, online communities.

Gate: 5+ phenomena from 3+ domains
02

Tension Mining

Identify forces pulling the system in different directions. A tension is a tradeoff that cannot be permanently eliminated — it can only be managed. Not "pros vs cons." Ineliminable tradeoffs.

Gate: 5+ tensions with Force A/B identified
03

Invariant Mining

Extract principles that appear across multiple tensions and multiple domains. Invariants are the "laws" of complex systems — they explain why the same tension patterns appear in biology, society, and technology.

Gate: 3+ cross-domain invariants with boundary conditions
04

Mechanism Mining

Study how reality resolves the tensions you identified. Nature, society, and technology have already developed solutions. Your job is to find and understand them.

Gate: 3+ mechanisms mapped to tensions
05

System Synthesis

Combine tensions, invariants, and mechanisms into a coherent model. Identify which tensions are primary, which mechanisms are essential, and what failure modes exist.

Gate: Coherent system model with predicted behaviors
06

Algorithm Synthesis

Only now design algorithms. Derive them from the mechanisms, not from first principles. If your mechanisms are well understood, the algorithm should emerge naturally.

Gate: Algorithm candidates mapped to Phase 4 mechanisms
07

Destruction Phase

Attack the entire model. Assume it is wrong. Identify weak assumptions, missing tensions, missing invariants, and possible redesigns. No analysis is complete without this.

Gate: Failure analysis + D1-D5 self-evaluation
